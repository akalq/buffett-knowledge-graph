伯克希尔·哈撒韦公司

致伯克希尔·哈撒韦公司的股东：

English
Charlie Munger, my long-time partner, and I have the job of managing the savings of a great number of individuals. We are grateful for their enduring trust, a relationship that often spans much of their adult lifetime. It is those dedicated savers that are forefront in my mind as I write this letter.
中文翻译
我的长期合作伙伴查理·芒格和我，负责管理大量个人的储蓄工作。我们感谢他们持久的信任，这种关系通常贯穿他们成年后的大部分人生。当我写这封信时，这些忠实的储蓄者是我心中最重要的人。
English
A common belief is that people choose to save when young, expecting thereby to maintain their living standards after retirement. Any assets that remain at death, this theory says, will usually be left to their families or, possibly, to friends and philanthropy. Our experience has differed. We believe Berkshire's individual holders largely to be of the once-a-saver, always-a-saver variety. Though these people live well, they eventually dispense most of their funds to philanthropic organizations. These, in turn, redistribute the funds by expenditures intended to improve the lives of a great many people who are unrelated to the original benefactor. Sometimes, the results have been spectacular.
中文翻译
一种普遍的看法是，人们在年轻时选择储蓄，希望因此在退休后维持生活水平。该理论认为，任何在死亡时留下的资产通常会留给家人，或者可能留给朋友和慈善机构。我们的经验与此不同。我们相信伯克希尔的个人股东大多是一旦储蓄、始终储蓄的类型。虽然这些人生活得很好，但他们最终会将大部分资金分配给慈善机构。这些机构转而通过旨在改善与原始捐赠者无关的大量人民生活的支出来重新分配资金。有时，结果是惊人的。
English
The disposition of money unmasks humans. Charlie and I watch with pleasure the vast flow of Berkshire-generated funds to public needs and, alongside, the infrequency with which our shareholders opt for look-at-me assets and dynasty-building. Who wouldn't enjoy working for shareholders like ours?
中文翻译
金钱的处置方式揭露人性。查理和我欣慰地看到大量伯克希尔产生的资金流向公共需求，同时也注意到我们的股东很少选择炫耀性资产和王朝建设。谁不愿意为这样的股东工作呢？

我们做什么

English
Charlie and I allocate your savings at Berkshire between two related forms of ownership. First, we invest in businesses that we control, usually buying 100% of each. Berkshire directs capital allocation at these subsidiaries and selects the CEOs who make day-by-day operating decisions. When large enterprises are being managed, both trust and rules are essential. Berkshire emphasizes the former to an unusual – some would say extreme – degree. Disappointments are inevitable. We are understanding about business mistakes; our tolerance for personal misconduct is zero.
中文翻译
查理和我将您在伯克希尔的储蓄分配为两种相关的所有权形式。首先，我们投资于我们控制的企业，通常购买每家企业的100%股权。伯克希尔指导这些子公司的资本配置，并选择做出日常运营决策的CEO。在管理大型企业时，信任和规则都是必不可少的。伯克希尔以不寻常的——有人会说极端的——程度强调前者。失望是不可避免的。我们理解业务错误；但我们对个人不当行为的容忍度为零。
English
In our second category of ownership, we buy publicly-traded stocks through which we passively own pieces of businesses. Holding these investments, we have no say in management. Our goal in both forms of ownership is to make meaningful investments in businesses with both long-lasting favorable economic characteristics and trustworthy managers. Please note particularly that we own publicly-traded stocks based on our expectations about their long-term business performance, not because we view them as vehicles for adroit purchases and sales. That point is crucial: Charlie and I are not stock-pickers; we are business-pickers.
中文翻译
在第二种所有权形式中，我们购买公开交易的股票，通过这些股票被动地拥有企业的一部分。持有这些投资时，我们对管理没有发言权。两种所有权形式的目标都是对具有持久有利经济特征和可信管理者的企业进行有意义的投资。请特别注意，我们持有公开交易的股票是基于对其长期业务表现的预期，而不是因为我们将它们视为巧妙买卖的工具。这一点至关重要：查理和我不是选股者；我们是选企业者。
English
Over the years, I have made many mistakes. Consequently, our extensive collection of businesses currently consists of a few enterprises that have truly extraordinary economics, many enterprises that enjoy very good economic characteristics, and a large group that are marginal. Along the way, other businesses in which I have invested have died, their products unwanted by the public. Capitalism has two sides: The system creates an ever-growing pile of losers while concurrently delivering a gusher of improved goods and services. Schumpeter called this phenomenon "creative destruction."
中文翻译
多年来，我犯了很多错误。因此，我们庞大的企业集合目前由少数具有真正卓越经济特征的企业、许多具有非常好经济特征的企业和一大群处于边缘的企业组成。在这一过程中，我投资过的其他企业已经消亡，其产品被公众抛弃。资本主义有两面性：这个体系在创造越来越多的失败者的同时，也同时提供着大量改善的商品和服务。熊彼特称这种现象为"创造性破坏"。
English
One advantage of our publicly-traded segment is that – episodically – it becomes easy to buy pieces of wonderful businesses at wonderful prices. It's crucial to understand that stocks often trade at truly foolish prices, both high and low. "Efficient" markets exist only in textbooks. In truth, marketable stocks and bonds are baffling, their behavior usually understandable only in retrospect.
中文翻译
我们公开交易部分的一个优势是——偶尔——可以很容易地以出色的价格购买优秀企业的部分股份。关键是要理解，股票经常以真正愚蠢的价格交易，无论是高还是低。"有效"市场只存在于教科书中。事实上，可流通股票和债券是令人困惑的，其行为通常只有在回顾时才能理解。
English
Controlled businesses are a different breed. They sometimes command ridiculously higher prices than justified but are almost never available at bargain valuations. Unless under duress, the owner of a controlled business gives no thought to selling at a panic-type valuation.
中文翻译
控股企业是另一种类型。它们有时会以荒谬地高于合理的价格叫卖，但几乎从不以廉价估值出售。除非在压力下，控股企业的所有者不会考虑以恐慌型估值出售。
English
At this point, a report card from me is appropriate: In 58 years of Berkshire management, most of my capital-allocation decisions have been no better than so-so. In some cases, also, bad moves by me have been rescued by very large doses of luck. (Remember our escapes from near-disasters at US Air and Salomon? I certainly do.) Our satisfactory results have been the product of about a dozen truly good decisions – that would be about one every five years – and a sometimes-forgotten advantage that favors long-term investors such as Berkshire. Let's take a peek behind the curtain.
中文翻译
在这个时候，我应该给自己一份成绩单：在58年的伯克希尔管理生涯中，我的大多数资本配置决策不过是马马虎虎。在某些情况下，我的糟糕举动也被大量的运气所拯救。（还记得我们在美航和所罗门的近乎灾难中逃脱吗？我当然记得。）我们令人满意的结果是大约十几个真正好的决策的产物——大约每五年一个——以及一个有时被遗忘的优势，这种优势有利于伯克希尔等长期投资者。让我们看看幕后。

秘密配方

English
In August 1994 – yes, 1994 – Berkshire completed its seven-year purchase of the 400 million shares of Coca-Cola we now own. The total cost was $1.3 billion – then a very meaningful sum at Berkshire. The cash dividend we received from Coke in 1994 was $75 million. By 2022, the dividend had increased to $704 million. Growth occurred every year, just as certain as birthdays. All Charlie and I were required to do was cash Coke's quarterly dividend checks. We expect that those checks are highly likely to grow.
中文翻译
1994年8月——是的，1994年——伯克希尔完成了对我们现在拥有的4亿股可口可乐的七年购买。总成本为13亿美元——当时对伯克希尔来说是一笔非常有意义的金额。我们1994年从可口可乐收到的现金股息为7500万美元。到2022年，股息已增长到7.04亿美元。每年都有增长，就像生日一样确定。查理和我需要做的只是兑现可口可乐的季度股息支票。我们预计这些支票很可能继续增长。
English
American Express is much the same story. Berkshire's purchases of Amex were essentially completed in 1995 and, coincidentally, also cost $1.3 billion. Annual dividends received from this investment have grown from $41 million to $302 million. Those checks, too, seem highly likely to increase.
中文翻译
美国运通的故事大致相同。伯克希尔对运通的收购基本上在1995年完成，巧合的是，成本也是13亿美元。从这项投资中收到的年度股息从4100万美元增长到3.02亿美元。这些支票似乎也非常有可能增加。
English
These dividend gains, though pleasing, are far from spectacular. But they bring with them important gains in stock prices. At yearend, our Coke investment was valued at $25 billion while Amex was recorded at $22 billion. Each holding now accounts for roughly 5% of Berkshire's net worth, akin to its weighting long ago.
中文翻译
这些股息增长虽然令人高兴，但远非惊人。但它们伴随着重要的股价收益。年底时，我们持有的可口可乐投资价值250亿美元，而运通记录为220亿美元。每项持股现在约占伯克希尔净资产的5%，与很久以前的权重相似。
English
The lesson for investors: The weeds wither away in significance as the flowers bloom. Over time, it takes just a few winners to work wonders. And, yes, it helps to start early and live into your 90s as well.
中文翻译
给投资者的教训：当花朵绽放时，杂草的重要性就会消退。随着时间的推移，只需要少数赢家就能创造奇迹。而且，是的早点开始并活到90多岁也有帮助。

去年概况

English
Berkshire had a good year in 2022. The company's operating earnings – our term for income calculated using Generally Accepted Accounting Principles ("GAAP"), exclusive of capital gains or losses from equity holdings – set a record at $30.8 billion. Charlie and I focus on this operational figure and urge you to do so as well. The GAAP figure, absent our adjustment, fluctuates wildly and capriciously at every reporting date.
中文翻译
伯克希尔在2022年表现良好。公司的运营收益——我们用来表示按照公认会计原则（"GAAP"）计算的收入，但不包括股权投资的资本收益或损失——创下308亿美元的纪录。查理和我专注于这个运营数字，并敦促您也这样做。GAAP数字，没有我们的调整，在每个报告日期都会剧烈且反复无常地波动。
English
A second positive development for Berkshire last year was our purchase of Alleghany Corporation, a property-casualty insurer captained by Joe Brandon. I've worked with Joe in the past, and he understands both Berkshire and insurance. Alleghany delivers special value to us because Berkshire's unmatched financial strength allows its insurance subsidiaries to follow valuable and enduring investment strategies unavailable to virtually all competitors.
中文翻译
去年对伯克希尔的第二个积极进展是我们收购了Alleghany公司，这是一家由乔·布兰登领导的财产/意外险公司。我过去与乔合作过，他了解伯克希尔和保险。Alleghany为我们提供了特殊价值，因为伯克希尔无与伦比的财务实力使其保险子公司能够遵循对几乎所有竞争对手都不可用的有价值且持久的投资策略。
English
Aided by Alleghany, our insurance float increased during 2022 from $147 billion to $164 billion. With disciplined underwriting, these funds have a decent chance of being cost-free over time. Since purchasing our first property-casualty insurer in 1967, Berkshire's float has increased 8,000-fold through acquisitions, operations and innovations. Though not recognized in our financial statements, this float has been an extraordinary asset for Berkshire.
中文翻译
在Alleghany的帮助下，我们的保险浮存金在2022年从1470亿美元增加到1640亿美元。通过严格的核保，这些资金有很大的机会随着时间推移是零成本的。自1967年收购我们的第一家财产/意外险公司以来，伯克希尔的浮存金通过收购、运营和创新增长了8000倍。虽然在我们的财务报表中没有确认，但浮存金一直是伯克希尔的一笔非凡资产。
English
Gains from value-accretive repurchases, it should be emphasized, benefit all owners – in every respect. Imagine, if you will, three fully-informed shareholders of a local auto dealership, one of whom manages the business. Imagine, further, that one of the passive owners wishes to sell his interest back to the company at a price attractive to the two continuing shareholders. When completed, has this transaction harmed anyone? Is the manager somehow favored over the continuing passive owners? Has the public been hurt?
中文翻译
应该强调的是，来自价值增值型回购的收益在各方面都使所有股东受益。想象一下，一个当地汽车经销店的三个完全知情的股东，其中一人管理业务。再想象一下，其中一个被动股东希望以对两个继续持股股东有吸引力的价格将他的权益卖回给公司。完成后，这笔交易伤害了任何人吗？经理是否以某种方式偏爱于继续持股的被动股东？公众受到伤害了吗？
English
When you are told that all repurchases are harmful to shareholders or to the country, or particularly beneficial to CEOs, you are listening to either an economic illiterate or a silver-tongued demagogue (characters that are not mutually exclusive).
中文翻译
当你被告知所有回购都对股东或国家有害，或者特别有利于CEO时，你听到的要么是经济文盲，要么是花言巧语的煽动者（这两种角色并不互相排斥）。

58年——和一些数字

English
In 1965, Berkshire was a one-trick pony, the owner of a venerable – but doomed – New England textile operation. With that business on a death march, Berkshire needed an immediate fresh start. Looking back, I was slow to recognize the severity of its problems. And then came a stroke of good luck: National Indemnity became available in 1967, and we shifted our resources toward insurance and other non-textile operations.
中文翻译
1965年，伯克希尔是一匹独马拥有一家历史悠久但注定要消亡的新英格兰纺织业务。随着该业务走上死亡行军，伯克希尔需要立即重新开始。回想起来，我迟迟没有认识到其问题的严重性。然后运气来了：1967年，国家保险公司出现了，我们将资源转向保险和其他非纺织业务。
English
Thus began our journey to 2023, a bumpy road involving a combination of continuous savings by our owners (that is, by their retaining earnings), the power of compounding, our avoidance of major mistakes and – most important of all – the American Tailwind. America would have done fine without Berkshire. The reverse is not true.
中文翻译
因此开始了我们到2023年的旅程，这是一条坎坷的道路，涉及所有者持续储蓄（即他们保留收益）、复利的力量、我们避免重大错误，以及——最重要的是——美国顺风。如果没有伯克希尔，美国会做得很好。反过来就不成立了。
English
At yearend 2022, Berkshire was the largest owner of eight of these giants: American Express, Bank of America, Chevron, Coca-Cola, HP Inc., Moody's, Occidental Petroleum and Paramount Global. In addition to those eight investees, Berkshire owns 100% of BNSF and 92% of BH Energy, each with earnings that exceed the $3 billion mark noted above.
中文翻译
到2022年底，伯克希尔是这八家巨头中最大的所有者：美国运通、美国银行、雪佛龙、可口可乐、惠普公司、穆迪、西方石油和派拉蒙全球。除这八家被投资单位外，伯克希尔还拥有BNSF 100%的股份和BH能源92%的股份，每家公司的收益都超过上述30亿美元的门槛。
English
As for the future, Berkshire will always hold a boatload of cash and U.S. Treasury bills along with a wide array of businesses. We will also avoid behavior that could result in any uncomfortable cash needs at inconvenient times, including financial panics and unprecedented insurance losses. Our CEO will always be the Chief Risk Officer – a task it is irresponsible to delegate. Additionally, our future CEOs will have a significant part of their net worth in Berkshire shares, bought with their own money. And yes, our shareholders will continue to save and prosper by retaining earnings. At Berkshire, there will be no finish line.
中文翻译
至于未来，伯克希尔将始终持有大量现金和美国国债以及各种业务。我们也将避免任何可能在不便利的时候导致令人不安的现金需求的行为，包括金融恐慌和前所未有的保险损失。我们的CEO将始终担任首席风险官——这是一项不负责任地委托他人的任务。此外，我们未来的CEO将以其自有资金购买的伯克希尔股票作为其净资产的重要组成部分。是的，我们的股东将继续通过保留收益来储蓄和繁荣。在伯克希尔，没有终点线。

关于联邦税收的一些令人惊讶的事实

English
During the decade ending in 2021, the United States Treasury received about $32.3 trillion in taxes while it spent $43.9 trillion. Though economists, politicians and many of the public have opinions about the consequences of that huge imbalance, Charlie and I plead ignorance and firmly believe that near-term economic and market forecasts are worse than useless.
中文翻译
在截至2021年的十年中，美国财政部收到了约32.3万亿美元的税收，而支出为43.9万亿美元。虽然经济学家、政治家和许多公众对这个巨大不平衡的后果有看法，但查理和我承认无知，并坚信对短期经济和市场的预测弊大于利。
English
And that means – brace yourself – had there been roughly 1,000 taxpayers in the U.S. matching Berkshire's payments, no other businesses nor any of the country's 131 million households would have needed to pay any taxes to the federal government. Not a dime.
中文翻译
这意味着——请站稳——如果美国大约有1000名纳税人支付与伯克希尔相同的税款，其他所有企业和该国1.31亿户家庭都不需要向联邦政府缴纳任何税款。一分钱都不用。
English
When it comes to federal taxes, individuals who own Berkshire can unequivocally state "I gave at the office."
中文翻译
说到联邦所得税，拥有伯克希尔股票的个人可以明确地说："我在办公室已经付过了。"
English
At Berkshire we hope and expect to pay much more in taxes during the next decade. We owe the country no less: America's dynamism has made a huge contribution to whatever success Berkshire has achieved – a contribution Berkshire will always need. We count on the American Tailwind and, though it has been becalmed from time to time, its propelling force has always returned.
中文翻译
在伯克希尔，我们希望并预计在未来十年支付更多税款。我们欠国家不少：美国的活力为伯克希尔取得的任何成功做出了巨大贡献——伯克希尔将永远需要这种贡献。我们依靠美国顺风，虽然它不时停滞，但其推动力总是会回来。
English
I have been investing for 80 years – more than one-third of our country's lifetime. Despite our citizens' penchant – almost enthusiasm – for self-criticism and self-doubt, I have yet to see a time when it made sense to make a long-term bet against America. And I doubt very much that any reader of this letter will have a different experience in the future.
中文翻译
我已经投资了80年——超过我们国家寿命的三分之一。尽管我们的公民热衷于——几乎是热情地——自我批评和自我怀疑，但我还没有看到有任何时候做空美国是明智的。我非常怀疑这封信的任何读者在未来会有不同的经历。

没有什么比拥有一个伟大的合作伙伴更好的了

English
