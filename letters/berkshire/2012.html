保险业务

English
Property-casualty ("P/C") insurers receive premiums upfront and pay claims later. In extreme cases, such as those arising from certain workers' compensation accidents, payments can stretch over decades. This collect-now, pay-later model leaves us holding large sums – money we call "float" – that will eventually go to others. Meanwhile, we get to invest this float for Berkshire's benefit. Though individual policies and claims come and go, the amount of float we hold remains quite stable in relation to premium volume. Consequently, as our business grows, so does our float.
中文翻译
财产意外险（"P/C"）保险公司预先收取保费，稍后支付赔款。在极端情况下，比如某些工伤事故，付款可能延续数十年。这种"现在收取，以后支付"的模式使我们持有大量资金——我们称之为"浮存金"——这些钱最终将流向他人。与此同时，我们代表伯克希尔的利益投资这些浮存金。虽然单个保单和赔款来来去去，但我们持有的浮存金数量与保费规模相比保持稳定。因此，随着我们业务增长，我们的浮存金也增长。
English
If our premiums exceed the total of our expenses and eventual losses, we register an underwriting profit that adds to the investment income our float produces. When such a profit is earned, we enjoy the use of free money – and, better yet, get paid for holding it. That's the key to having the bank pay you interest.
中文翻译
如果我们的保费超过费用和最终损失的总和，我们就获得承保利润，这会增加浮存金产生的投资收益。当获得这种利润时，我们享受免费资金的使用——而且更好的是，还能因持有它而获得报酬。这就是让银行付给你利息的诀窍。
English
Unfortunately, the wish of all insurers to achieve this happy result creates intense competition, so vigorous in most years that it causes the P/C industry as a whole to operate at a significant underwriting loss. This loss, in effect, is what the industry pays to hold its float. For example, State Farm, by far the country's largest insurer and a well-managed company besides, incurred an underwriting loss in eight of the eleven years ending in 2011. (Their financials for 2012 are not yet available.) There are a lot of ways to lose money in insurance, and the industry never ceases searching for new ones.
中文翻译
