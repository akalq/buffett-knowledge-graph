The first component of value is our investments: stocks, bonds and cash equivalents. At yearend these totaled $158 billion at market value. Insurance float – money we temporarily hold in our insurance operations that does not belong to us – funds $66 billion of our investments. This float is "free" as long as insurance underwriting breaks even, meaning that the premiums we receive equal the losses and expenses we incur. Of course, underwriting results are volatile, swinging erratically between profits and losses. Over our entire history, though, we've been significantly profitable, and I also expect us to average breakeven results or better in the future. If we do that, all of our investments – those funded both by float and by retained earnings – can be viewed as an element of value for Berkshire shareholders.
中文翻译

English
Property-casualty ("P/C") insurers receive premiums upfront and pay claims later. In extreme cases, such as those arising from certain workers' compensation accidents, payments can stretch over decades. This collect-now, pay-later model leaves us holding large sums – money we call "float" – that will eventually go to others. Meanwhile, we get to invest this float for Berkshire's benefit. Though individual policies and claims come and go, amount of float we hold remains remarkably stable in relation to premium volume. Consequently, as our business grows, so does our float.
中文翻译
财产意外险（"P/C"）保险公司预先收取保费，稍后支付赔款。在极端情况下，比如某些工伤事故，付款可能延续数十年。这种"现在收取，以后支付"的模式使我们持有大量资金——我们称之为"浮存金"——这些钱最终将流向他人。与此同时，我们代表伯克希尔的利益投资这些浮存金。虽然单个保单和赔款来来去去，但我们持有的浮存金数量与保费规模相比保持稳定。因此，随着我们业务增长，我们的浮存金也增长。
English
If our premiums exceed the total of our expenses and eventual losses, we register an underwriting profit that adds to the investment income our float produces. When such a profit occurs, we enjoy the use of free money – and, better yet, get paid for holding it. Alas, the wish of all insurers to achieve this happy result creates intense competition, so vigorous in most years that it causes the P/C industry as a whole to operate at a significant underwriting loss. This loss, in effect, is what the industry pays to hold its float.
中文翻译
