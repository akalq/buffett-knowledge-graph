伯克希尔·哈撒韦公司

致伯克希尔·哈撒韦公司的股东：

English
This letter comes to you as part of Berkshire's annual report. As a public company, we are required to periodically tell you many specific facts and figures. "Report," however, implies a greater responsibility. In addition to mandated data, we believe we owe you additional commentary about what you own and how we think. Our goal is to communicate with you in a manner that we would wish you to use if our positions were reversed – that is, if you were Berkshire's CEO while I and my family were passive investors, trusting you with our savings.
中文翻译
这封信作为伯克希尔年度报告的一部分呈交给您。作为一家上市公司，我们需要定期向您提供许多具体的事实和数据。然而，"报告"一词意味着更大的责任。除了必须提供的数据外，我们认为有义务向您提供额外的评论，说明您拥有什么以及我们的想法。我们的目标是以一种我们希望您在相反情况下会使用的方式与您沟通——也就是说，如果您是伯克希尔的CEO，而我和我的家人是被动的投资者，将储蓄托付给您。
English
This approach leads us to an annual recitation of both good and bad developments at the many businesses you indirectly own through your Berkshire shares. When discussing problems at specific subsidiaries, we do, however, try to follow the advice Tom Murphy gave to me 60 years ago: "praise by name, criticize by category."
中文翻译
这种方法促使我们每年重述您通过伯克希尔股票间接拥有的许多企业的好坏发展。然而，在讨论特定子公司的问题时，我们确实努力遵循60年前汤姆·墨菲给我的建议："点名表扬，归类批评。"

错误——是的，我们在伯克希尔也会犯错

English
Sometimes I've made mistakes in assessing the future economics of a business I've purchased for Berkshire – each a case of capital allocation gone wrong. That happens with both judgments about marketable equities – we view these as partial ownership of businesses – and the 100% acquisitions of companies. At other times, I've made mistakes when assessing the abilities or fidelity of managers Berkshire is hiring. The fidelity disappointments can hurt beyond their financial impact, a pain that can approach that of a failed marriage.
中文翻译
有时，我在评估为伯克希尔购买的企业的未来经济时会犯错——每次都是资本配置错误的情况。这在判断可流通股票时会发生——我们将这些视为企业的部分所有权——以及在100%收购公司时也是如此。在其他时候，我在评估伯克希尔雇佣的管理者的能力或忠诚度时会犯错。忠诚度失望造成的伤害可能超过其财务影响，这种痛苦接近于婚姻失败的痛苦。
English
A decent batting average in personnel decisions is all that can be hoped for. The cardinal sin is delaying the correction of mistakes or what Charlie Munger called "thumb-sucking." Problems, he would tell me, cannot be wished away. They require action, however uncomfortable that may be.
中文翻译
在人事决策中，及格的平均水平就是能够期望的全部。大罪是延迟纠正错误或查理·芒格所说的"吮吸指"。他告诉我，问题无法通过愿望解决。无论多么不舒服，都需要采取行动。

皮特·利格尔——独一无二

English
Let me pause to tell you the remarkable story of Pete Liegl, a man unknown to most Berkshire shareholders but one who contributed many billions to their aggregate wealth. Pete died in November, still working at 80.
中文翻译
让我暂停一下，告诉您皮特·利格尔的非凡故事，这个人大多数伯克希尔股东都不认识，但他为他们的总财富贡献了数十亿美元。皮特于11月去世，时年80岁，仍在工作。
English
I first heard of Forest River – the Indiana company Pete founded and managed – on June 21, 2005. On that day I received a letter from an intermediary detailing relevant data about the company, a recreational vehicle ("RV") manufacturer. The writer said that Pete, the 100% owner of Forest River, specifically wanted to sell to Berkshire. He also told me the price that Pete expected to receive. I liked this no-nonsense approach.
中文翻译
我第一次听说森林河公司——皮特创立和管理的印第安纳州公司——是在2005年6月21日。那天，我收到一位中介的信，详细介绍了这家休闲车（"RV"）制造商的相关数据。写信人说，皮特作为森林河100%的所有者，特别希望卖给伯克希尔。他还告诉了我皮特期望收到的价格。我喜欢这种直截了当的方式。
English
I did some checking with RV dealers, liked what I learned and arranged a June 28th meeting in Omaha. Pete brought along his wife, Sharon, and daughter, Lisa. When we met, Pete assured me that he wanted to keep running the business but would feel more comfortable if he could assure financial security for his family.
中文翻译
我向一些休闲车经销商进行了核实，喜欢我了解到的内容，并安排在6月28日在奥马哈举行会议。皮特带来了他的妻子莎伦和女儿丽莎。当我们见面时，皮特向我保证，他希望继续经营业务，但如果能够为家庭确保经济安全感，他会更放心。
English
Pete next mentioned that he owned some real estate that was leased to Forest River and had not been covered in the June 21 letter. Within a few minutes, we arrived at a price for those assets as I expressed no need for appraisal by Berkshire but would simply accept his valuation. Then we arrived at the other point that needed clarity. I asked Pete what his compensation should be, adding that whatever he said, I would accept.
中文翻译
皮特接着提到，他拥有一些租赁给森林河的房地产，这些在6月21日的信中没有涵盖。几分钟内，我们就这些资产达成了价格，我表示不需要伯克希尔进行评估，直接接受他的估值。然后，我们需要明确的另一点。我问皮特他的薪酬应该多少，补充说无论他说什么，我都会接受。
English
Pete paused as his wife, daughter and I leaned forward. Then he surprised us: "Well, I looked at Berkshire's proxy statement and I wouldn't want to make more than my boss, so pay me $100,000 per year." After I picked myself off the floor, Pete added: "But we will earn X (he named a number) this year, and I would like an annual bonus of 10% of any earnings above what the company is now delivering." I replied: "OK Pete, but if Forest River makes any significant acquisitions we will make an appropriate adjustment for the additional capital thus employed."
中文翻译
皮特停顿了一下，他的妻子、女儿和我都向前倾身。然后，他让我们感到惊讶："嗯，我看了伯克希尔的委托书，我不想比我的老板赚得更多，所以每年付我10万美元。"当我从地上爬起来后，皮特补充道："但我们今年会赚到X（他叫了一个数字），我希望对超过公司目前交付的收益部分获得10%的年度奖金。"我回答："好的，皮特，但如果森林河进行任何重大收购，我们将对因此使用的额外资本进行适当调整。"
English
The four of us then went to dinner at Omaha's Happy Hollow Club and lived happily ever after. During the next 19 years, Pete shot the lights out. No competitor came close to his performance.
中文翻译
我们四个人随后去奥马哈的快乐空心俱乐部共进晚餐，从此幸福地生活。在接下来的19年里，皮特的表现让所有人黯然失色。没有竞争对手能接近他的表现。

去年的业绩

English
In 2024, Berkshire did better than I expected though 53% of our 189 operating businesses reported a decline in earnings. We were aided by a predictable large gain in investment income as Treasury Bill yields improved and we substantially increased our holdings of these highly-liquid short-term securities. Our insurance business also delivered a major increase in earnings, led by the performance of GEICO.
中文翻译
2024年，伯克希尔的表现比我预期的要好，尽管我们189家运营企业中有53%报告收益下降。我们得益于国债收益率提高带来的可预测的巨额投资收益增长，以及我们大幅增加了这些高流动性短期证券的持仓。我们的保险业务也带来了重大收益增长，主要得益于GEICO的表现。
English
In five years, Todd Combs has reshaped GEICO in a major way, increasing efficiency and bringing underwriting practices up to date. GEICO was a long-held gem that needed major repolishing, and Todd has worked tirelessly in getting the job done. Though not yet complete, the 2024 improvement was spectacular.
中文翻译
五年来，托德·康姆斯以重大方式重塑了GEICO，提高了效率并更新了承保做法。GEICO是我们长期持有的一颗宝石，需要大修，而托德在完成这项工作上不知疲倦地努力着。虽然尚未完全完成，但2024年的改进是惊人的。
English
In general, property-casualty ("P/C") insurance pricing strengthened during 2024, reflecting a major increase in damage from convective storms. Climate change may have been announcing its arrival. However, no "monster" event occurred during 2024. Someday, any day, a truly staggering insurance loss will occur – and there is no guarantee that there will be only one per annum.
中文翻译
总体而言，财产/意外险（"P/C"）保险定价在2024年有所加强，反映了对流风暴造成的损害大幅增加。气候变化可能正在宣告它的到来。然而，2024年没有发生"怪物"级事件。总有一天，任何一天，都会发生真正惊人的保险损失——无法保证每年只有一次。
English
Berkshire's railroad and utility operations, our two largest businesses outside of insurance, improved their aggregate earnings. Both, however, have much left to accomplish.
中文翻译
伯克希尔的铁路和公用事业运营，我们在保险之外的两家最大企业，提高了它们的合计收益。然而，两家都还有很多工作要完成。

您的资金在哪里

English
Berkshire's equity activity is ambidextrous. In one hand we own control of many businesses, holding at least 80% of the investee's shares. Generally, we own 100%. These 189 subsidiaries have similarities to marketable common stocks but are far from identical. The collection is worth many hundreds of billions and includes a few rare gems, many good-but-far-from-fabulous businesses and some laggards that have been disappointments.
中文翻译
伯克希尔的股权活动是双管齐下的。在一只手里，我们控制着许多企业，持有至少80%的被投资单位股份。通常，我们拥有100%。这189家子公司与可流通普通股有相似之处，但远非相同。这个组合价值数千亿美元，包括一些稀世珍宝、许多良好但远非凡的企业，以及一些令人失望的落后者。
English
In the other hand, we own a small percentage of a dozen or so very large and highly profitable businesses with household names such as Apple, American Express, Coca-Cola and Moody's. Many of these companies earn very high returns on the net tangible equity required for their operations. At yearend, our partial-ownership holdings were valued at $272 billion. Understandably, really outstanding businesses are very seldom offered in their entirety, but small fractions of these gems can be purchased Monday through Friday on Wall Street and, very occasionally, they sell at bargain prices.
中文翻译
在另一只手里，我们拥有十几家左右非常庞大且高度盈利的企业的小比例股权，这些企业包括苹果、美国运通、可口可乐和穆迪等家喻户晓的名字。这些许多公司在运营所需的净有形股权上获得了非常高的回报。年底时，我们的部分所有权持股价值为2720亿美元。可以理解，真正卓越的企业几乎从不整体出售，但这些珍宝的小部分可以在周一至周五的华尔街购买，而且偶尔会以廉价出售。
English
We are impartial in our choice of equity vehicles, investing in either variety based upon where we can best deploy your (and my family's) savings. Often, nothing looks compelling; very infrequently we find ourselves knee-deep in opportunities. Greg has vividly shown his ability to act at such times as did Charlie. With marketable equities, it is easier to change course when I make a mistake.
中文翻译
我们在选择股权工具时是公正的，根据我们认为最能部署您（和我家人的）储蓄的地方，投资于任何一种形式。通常，没有什么看起来吸引人；很少我们会发现自己深入机会之中。格雷格在这样的时刻展现行动的能力，就像查理过去一样。对于可流通股票，当我犯错时更容易改变方向。
English
Berkshire's present size, it should be underscored, diminishes this valuable option. We can't come and go on a dime. Sometimes a year or more is required to establish or divest an investment. Additionally, with ownership of minority positions we can't change management if that action is needed or control what is done with capital flows if we are unhappy with the decisions being made. With controlled companies, we can dictate these decisions, but we have far less flexibility in the disposition of mistakes.
中文翻译
应该强调的是，伯克希尔目前的规模削弱了这一宝贵的选择。我们不能来去自如。有时需要一年或更多时间才能建立或退出一项投资。此外，对于少数股权的所有权，如果需要改变管理层，我们无法这样做；如果我们对资本流动的决策不满意，我们也无法控制。对于控股公司，我们可以决定这些决策，但在处理错误时灵活性要少得多。
English
Despite what some commentators currently view as an extraordinary cash position at Berkshire, the great majority of your money remains in equities. That preference won't change. While our ownership in marketable equities moved downward last year from $354 billion to $272 billion, the value of our non-quoted controlled equities increased somewhat and remains far greater than the value of the marketable portfolio.
中文翻译
尽管一些评论员目前认为伯克希尔持有异常大量的现金，但您的资金大部分仍在股权中。这一偏好不会改变。虽然我们对可流通股票的所有权从3540亿美元下降到2720亿美元，但我们未上市的控股股权价值有所增加，而且仍然远高于可流通投资组合的价值。

财产意外险保险

English
P/C insurance continues to be Berkshire's core business. The industry follows a financial model that is rare – very rare – among giant businesses. Customarily, companies incur costs for labor, materials, inventories, plant and equipment, etc. before – or concurrently with – the sale of their products or services. Consequently, their CEOs have a good fix on knowing the cost of their product before they sell it. If the selling price is less than its cost, managers soon learn they have a problem. Hemorrhaging cash is hard to ignore.
中文翻译
财产/意外险保险仍然是伯克希尔的核心业务。该行业遵循一种在大型企业中罕见——非常罕见——的金融模式。通常，公司在销售其产品或服务之前或同时发生劳动、材料、库存、厂房和设备等成本。因此，他们的CEO在销售之前就很好地知道产品的成本。如果销售价格低于成本，经理很快就会意识到有问题。现金大量外流是难以忽视的。
English
When writing P/C insurance, we receive payment upfront and much later learn what our product has cost us – sometimes a moment of truth that is delayed as much as 30 or more years. This mode of operations has the desirable effect of giving P/C insurers cash before they incur most expenses but carries with it the risk that the company can be losing money – sometimes mountains of money – before the CEO and directors realize what is happening.
中文翻译
在承保财产/意外险保险时，我们先收到付款，而很久以后才知道我们的产品成本——有时这一真相时刻可能延迟30年或更长时间。这种运营模式具有可取的效果，即在财产/意外险保险公司产生大部分费用之前就给他们现金，但同时也伴随着风险：公司可能在CEO和董事意识到之前就已经亏损——有时是巨额损失。

伯克希尔增加其在日本的投资

English
A small but important exception to our U.S.-based focus is our growing investment in Japan. It's been almost six years since Berkshire began purchasing shares in five Japanese companies that very successfully operate in a manner somewhat similar to Berkshire itself. The five are (alphabetically) ITOCHU, Marubeni, Mitsubishi, Mitsui and Sumitomo. Each of these large enterprises, in turn, owns interests in a vast array of businesses, many based in Japan but others that operate throughout the world.
中文翻译
我们以美国为中心的重点有一个小但重要的例外，那就是我们在日本的不断增长的投资。自伯克希尔开始购买五家日本公司的股票以来已经近六年了，这五家公司以与伯克希尔本身有点相似的方式非常成功运营。这五家公司（按字母顺序）是伊藤忠、丸红、三菱、三井和住友。这大型企业中的每家又拥有一系列庞大企业中的权益，其中许多总部在日本，但也有些在全球各地运营。
English
Berkshire made its first purchases involving the five in July 2019. We simply looked at their financial records and were amazed at the low prices of their stocks. As the years have passed, our admiration for these companies has consistently grown. Greg has met many times with them, and I regularly follow their progress. Both of us like their capital deployment, their managements and their attitude in respect to their investors. Each of the five companies increase dividends when appropriate, they repurchase their shares when it is sensible to do so, and their top managers are far less aggressive in their compensation programs than their U.S. counterparts.
中文翻译
伯克希尔在2019年7月首次购买涉及这五家公司的股票。我们只是查看了它们的财务记录，对其股票的低价格感到惊讶。随着岁月流逝，我们对这些公司的敬佩与日俱增。格雷格多次与他们见面，我也定期跟进他们的进展。我们俩都喜欢他们的资本配置、管理层和对待投资者的态度。这五家公司都会在适当时候增加股息，在明智的时候回购股票，而且他们的高层管理人员在薪酬计划上远不如美国同行那样激进。
English
Our holdings of the five are for the very long term, and we are committed to supporting their boards of directors. From the start, we also agreed to keep Berkshire's holdings below 10% of each company's shares. But, as we approached this limit, the five companies agreed to moderately relax the ceiling. Over time, you will likely see Berkshire's ownership of all five increase somewhat.
中文翻译
我们对这五家公司的持股是非常长期的，我们承诺支持它们的董事会。从一开始，我们也同意将伯克希尔的持股保持在每家公司股票的10%以下。但随着我们接近这一限额，这五家公司同意适度放宽上限。随着时间的推移，您很可能会看到伯克希尔对这五家公司的所有权都会有所增加。
